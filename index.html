Olmsted County Nonprofit Housing Services Timeline

Olmsted County is home to many nonprofits supporting the housing stability of Olmsted County residents. This timeline is a collection of information from local nonprofit organizations that support housing needs in our community. Along the housing continuum there are many types of services and many organizations offering support. We have organized this information by the type of housing service offered, specifically focusing on day centers and emergency sheltering.

We would like to acknowledge that the organizations represented on this timeline are organizations that we were able to collect information from or about thus our data is not all inclusive. If you would like your organization and program added, please contact the Community Impact team at United Way of Olmsted County: impact@uwolmsted.org

The Landing

2018-Current

Serves: Individuals & Families

The Landing began as an outreach group connecting the homeless to the services of other agencies in town. Since 2020, they now offer a day center for homeless families and individuals to have a landing place until the overnight shelters open.

Salvation Army: Warming Center

2019-Current

Serves: Adults

The Salvation Army opened an emergency warming center in lieu of additional shelters in town. This overnight shelter was available when the temperature dropped below 0°F. The shelter was supplemental to Dorothy Day House and intended to be a temporary operation until another shelter was established in Rochester to support Dorothy Day. Catholic Charities opened the Rochester Community Warming Center in 2019, and the services of an emergency shelter through the Salvation Army was no longer needed.  Since 2019, the Salvation Army has operated as a day center for individuals needing a place to be during regular business hours.

Catholic Charities: Rochester Community Warming Center

2019-Current

Serves: Adults

The Rochester Community Warming Center opened in 2019 to serve homeless adults in Rochester, MN. This program provides overnight shelter, meals, showers, laundry, and connections to mainstream resources. Individuals utilizing this low barrier program do not need to meet particular requirements other than having a safety mindset and can stay as long as they need. The Warming Center can shelter up to 42 individuals each night.

Dorothy Day Hospitality House

1984-Current

Serves: Adults (& Occasionally Families)

The Dorothy Day Hospitality House has been in Rochester for over 35 years. This nationwide program has an incredible legacy across the United States. The Rochester location has 23 beds set up in a dormitory style. Individuals are able to stay 14 days. After a stay, people must wait two months before they are able to access these services again. Requirements to stay include identification, sobriety, and an agreement to abide by the House Rules.

Family Promise Rochester

2001-Current

Serves: Families

Family Promise Rochester began 20 years ago as Interfaith Hospitality Network, a group of churches working together to provide food and shelter for homeless families. The work of these early volunteers has helped the growth of the organization to become Family Promise Rochester. The shelter program is goal-oriented and families enter when they are ready to make lasting change for themselves and their families. In 2006, the organization rebranded to Family Promise Rochester and joined a nationwide organization with over 200 affiliates. Even with the name change, they continue to utilize churches to provide shelter. In late 2020 they purchased a large, single-family home and opened a static site shelter, where the organization can provide shelter, case management, and other resources for up to 5 families under one roof.

The Salvation Army: Lodging Vouchers

Serves: Individuals & Families

For over 30 years, The Salvation Army has provided emergency hotel lodging for homeless individuals and families. Assistance is given as an overflow for the local overnight shelters or when the local shelter is not an option due to mobility needs. Emergency help is a 1-3 night stay with a safe plan in place for what will happen after the hotel stay. In late 2021, The Salvation Army received a one-time grant that allows families with children to have a longer temporary stay of up to 3 months.

The Salvation Army: Warming Center

2015-2019

Serves: Adults

The Salvation Army opened an emergency warming center in lieu of additional shelters in town. This overnight shelter was available when the temperature dropped below 0°F. The shelter was supplemental to Dorothy Day House and intended to be a temporary operation until another shelter was established in Rochester to support Dorothy Day. Catholic Charities opened the Rochester Community Warming Center in 2019, and the services of an emergency shelter through The Salvation Army was no longer needed. Since 2019, The Salvation Army now operates as a Day Center for individuals needing a place to be during regular business hours.

180 Degrees: VonWald Youth Center

1989-Current

Serves: Youth

The VonWald Youth Center (previously known as VonWald Group Home) has been serving youth (ages 12-19) experiencing homelessness in Olmsted County since 1989. Many youth utilizing this shelter option are experiencing a crisis due to family conflict. This safe housing option is the only homeless youth specific emergency shelter open 24/7 in the surrounding 22 county area. This shelter has 12 beds for teens and serves around 200 youth a year. Up until recently, it was required for youth to receive a county/state referral to stay at the VonWald Youth Shelter. With referrals no longer being necessary, the shelter is more accessible to youth in need. In 2011, the youth shelter was purchased by 180 Degrees.This is a Minnesota based organization that serves all youth regardless of gender and orientation who are at risk of homelessness. With new ownership, this program is able to access more resources and connections. They have now added outreach and a mobile case manager that supports youth at-risk of homelessness or that have previously attended the shelter.

Women’s Shelter and Support Center

1978-Current

Serves: Individuals & Families (specifically victims of domestic violence)

Women’s Shelter and Support Center (WSSC) began out of a grassroots movement materializing  as a 5 bedroom house known as the Marilyn Brodie House. Within the first 2 decades of operation WSSC expanded its physical capacity to shelter victims of violence. WSSC’s emergency shelter capacity depends on family size. This ranges from 29-40 individuals.
